# On Slop: A Field Guide for Journalists and Practitioners

**Solbakken Research Initiative · Working Paper · April 2026**

> "Most problematic AI-generated content is not false. It is produced by a process in which no actor bore meaningful cost for being wrong."

---

## What This Is

This is a working field guide attempting to define *slop* — the category of AI-generated and algorithmically produced content that is not quite misinformation, not hallucination, and not noise, but something the existing vocabulary cannot name precisely.

The core argument: slop is not a quality problem. It is an accountability problem. Content becomes slop when no feedback loop exists linking accuracy to outcome for any actor in its production chain.

The document is aimed at journalists, editors, fact-checkers, and practitioners dealing with this problem in live environments — where existing vocabulary keeps arriving slightly too late.

---

## The Core Definition

> **Slop** is content produced by a process in which no actor bore meaningful cost for inaccuracy — optimized instead for satisfying the formal expectations of its genre.

Meaningful cost takes concrete forms: editorial rejection, legal liability, reputational damage, loss of audience trust, or budgetary consequence.

---

## The Invariant Question

> Was anyone in the production chain in a position where being wrong would have cost them something real? If you cannot identify where that accountability resides, assume it does not exist.
